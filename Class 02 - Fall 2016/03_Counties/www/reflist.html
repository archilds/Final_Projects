Throughout the course of making this app, we drew inspiration from and/or adapted code from the following sources. In addition, many of this app's features are made possible by the R package authors we list below.

References

Data Sources

The following sources should be acknowledged for providing the data used in the pre-uploaded dataset. Details on which source each variable came from specifically can be found in the codebook.

  1. County level demographic variables come from the American Community Survey 5 Year Estimates file, 2010-2014, by the US Census Bureau.
  2. County Shapfiles, U.S. Census Bureau: https://www.census.gov/geo/maps-data/data/cbf/cbf_counties.html
  3. County level drug overdose rates come from the National Center for Health Statistics, https://blogs.cdc.gov/nchs-data-visualization/drug-poisoning-mortality/.
  4. County level election results come from The Guardian for 2012 (https://www.theguardian.com/news/datablog/2012/nov/07/us-2012-election-county-results-download) and Github for 2016 ( https://github.com/tonmcg/County_Level_Election_Results_12-16).
  5. Additional county level characteristics and health outcomes data were first assembled by the Health Inequality Project by Chetty et al. More information can be found at https://healthinequality.org/.
  6. The basemap in the mapping tab come from Open Street Map (http://www.openstreetmap.org/)
